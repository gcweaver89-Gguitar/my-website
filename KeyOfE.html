Key of E

What are the notes in the key of E?

E Major (E)


E F# G# A B C# D#



E Minor (Em)


E F# G A B C D



The "#" symbol represents a sharp note which means it's a half-step (one fret) higher than the natural note

You may come across a "b" symbol, this means that the note is a half setp lower than the natural note and will generally be used if you are descending a scale. There are only two sets of notes that will not have a sharp or flat in between them and they are E,F and B,C.
